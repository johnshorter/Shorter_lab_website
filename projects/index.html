Projects

Read about some of our current and past projects.

Depression & early-onset MDD

Genetic architecture of early- and late-onset depression Genome-wide association study across Nordic biobanks and registries

A collaboration led by John Shorter at Roskilde University with researchers across four Nordic countries, showing that early- and late-onset depression have partly distinct genetic architectures.

Thyroid function and early-onset depression A bidirectional Mendelian randomization study

Led by Triinu Peters at University Hospital Essen (with Anke Hinney’s group), in collaboration with John Shorter. A bidirectional two-sample Mendelian randomization study finding that causal links between thyroid function and depression are driven predominantly by early-onset MDD. Manuscript in preparation.

Thyroid & hypothyroidism

Hypothyroidism in the Danish Blood Donor Study Characterizing and modeling thyroid disease before diagnosis

In collaboration with Louise Torp Dalgaard at Roskilde University and Ole Birger Vesterager Pedersen (Department of Clinical Immunology, Zealand University Hospital). The project characterizes hypothyroidism in the DBDS and, in work led by PhD student Jonatan Langer, builds mathematical models of pre-diagnostic disease progression.

TSH × smoking interaction GWAS A genome-wide gene-by-environment study within the ThyroidOmics consortium

Part of the ThyroidOmics consortium, led by Alex Williams at the University of Leicester. A genome-wide SNP-by-smoking interaction study of thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH); the lab contributes a Danish Blood Donor Study analysis to be meta-analyzed with the UK Biobank.

Pharmacogenetics

Precision medicine in epilepsy Pharmacogenetics and therapeutic drug monitoring in treatment-resistant epilepsy

In collaboration with Dr. Allan Bayat at the Filadelfia epilepsy hospital in Dianalund, Denmark, using pharmacogenetics and therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM) to better understand and individualize antiepileptic treatment.

CYP2C19 and individualized drug response How CYP2C19 genetic variants shape clopidogrel efficacy in stroke patients

In collaboration with Michael Asger Andersen (Bispebjerg Hospital) and Morteza Vaez (Institute of Biological Psychiatry, Sct. Hans, Roskilde), studying how CYP2C19 genetic variants influence the efficacy and safety of clopidogrel in stroke patients.

Resources & tutorials

Tutorial on creating Polygenic Risk Scores A workshop presented at the Nordic Society of Human Genetics (NSHG)

A detailed guide on using the Polygenic Score Catalog and building polygenic risk scores with LDpred2.

In progress

Genome-wide association study of adolescent-onset depression In press at Nature Genetics

A genome-wide association study of adolescent-onset depression, accepted at Nature Genetics (preprint on medRxiv).
